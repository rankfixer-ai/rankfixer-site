Guide: Security

Data Security, IP Protection & Compliance When Hiring Filipino Virtual Assistants

NDAs, IP protection, managed networks, tax compliance, and legal considerations — everything businesses need to know before hiring offshore staff.

The Security Gap: Home-Based vs Facility-Based

The single biggest security vulnerability in offshore staffing is the uncontrolled environment. A home-based freelancer works on a residential internet connection, often on a personal device shared with family members. There is no network security, no physical access control, and no way to verify who else might access your data.

A facility-based VA works on a managed corporate network with firewalls, access policies, physical security, and direct supervision. The difference is the same as letting an employee work from a coffee shop versus a secured office.

Key Protections in a Managed Model

🔐

NDAs Under Philippine Law

Enforceable non-disclosure agreements signed as part of the employment contract — not a flimsy freelancer agreement with no legal weight.

📜

IP Assignment Clauses

All work product is legally assigned to you through employment contracts. Unlike freelancers, employees have clear IP assignment under Philippine labor law.

🖥

Managed Devices

Company-provided hardware with access controls, not personal laptops with unknown software and security configurations.

🌐

Controlled Network

Corporate-grade network with firewall protection, access logging, and physical security — not a residential WiFi router with default settings.

Philippine Employment Compliance: What Gets Handled

When you hire through a managed agency, all Philippine employment compliance is handled for you:

  • SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG: Mandatory government contributions processed monthly
  • 13th-month pay: Legally required benefit calculated and paid by the agency
  • Leave management: Service incentive leave, maternity/paternity leave, and other statutory benefits
  • Labor law compliance: Working hours, overtime, holiday pay — all managed per Philippine labor code
  • Tax withholding: Income tax calculated, withheld, and remitted to the BIR

Tax Implications for US, UK, and AU Businesses

When you contract with a Philippine staffing agency (not hire individuals directly), the relationship is B2B. You receive an invoice for services rendered. You do not become a Philippine employer. You do not need a Philippine entity. There is no permanent establishment risk from employing staff — because you aren't employing anyone; the agency is.

This is fundamentally different from hiring freelancers directly, where misclassification risk, permanent establishment concerns, and tax withholding obligations can become serious issues.
