11 SEO Tasks You Should Delegate to a VA (With Pay Ranges)

📅 May 19, 2026 ⏱️ 9 min read

You shouldn't be doing keyword research at 11 PM. You shouldn't be uploading blog posts, chasing link prospects, or filling out citation forms.

These tasks are necessary, but they're not where your value is as a business owner or strategist. A Filipino VA can handle them at $6-15/hour while you focus on client calls, strategy, and growth.

Here are 11 SEO tasks you should delegate immediately, with realistic hourly rates, skill levels needed, and what to look for when hiring.

1. Keyword Research

What they do: Find search terms your target audience uses, analyze search intent, assess difficulty, and organize keywords into topic clusters.

Hourly rate: $6-10/hour

Skill level: Entry to mid-level

Tools needed: Ahrefs, SEMrush, or Ubersuggest (you provide access)

What to look for: Someone who understands search intent (informational vs transactional), can identify long-tail variations, and knows how to filter by difficulty and volume.

Test task: "Find 20 keywords for a plumber in Austin, Texas. Group them by service type and search intent. Include difficulty scores and monthly volume."

2. Content Writing (SEO Blog Posts)

What they do: Write 1,500-2,500 word blog posts optimized for target keywords, formatted with H2/H3 headers, and structured for readability.

Hourly rate: $8-15/hour (or $0.03-0.08/word)

Skill level: Mid-level

Tools needed: SurferSEO, Grammarly, ChatGPT (optional)

What to look for: Strong English writing skills, ability to follow content briefs, understanding of on-page SEO (keyword placement, internal linking, meta descriptions).

Test task: "Write a 1,500-word blog post: 'How to Fix a Leaky Faucet.' Target keyword: 'how to fix leaky faucet.' Include 3 H2 sections, 2 internal links, and a meta description."

3. Content Uploading and Formatting

What they do: Upload blog posts to WordPress, format with proper headings, add images, insert internal links, optimize alt text, and publish or schedule.

Hourly rate: $5-8/hour

Skill level: Entry-level

Tools needed: WordPress, Yoast SEO plugin

What to look for: Experience with WordPress (Gutenberg or Classic Editor), basic HTML knowledge, attention to detail.

Test task: "Upload this Google Doc as a blog post. Add the featured image, optimize 3 images with alt text, insert 2 internal links, and set the meta description in Yoast."

4. On-Page SEO Optimization

What they do: Optimize existing pages for target keywords: update title tags, meta descriptions, H1/H2 headers, add schema markup, improve internal linking.

Hourly rate: $8-12/hour

Skill level: Mid-level

Tools needed: Screaming Frog, Yoast SEO, SurferSEO

What to look for: Understanding of on-page ranking factors, ability to audit pages and identify optimization opportunities, basic schema knowledge.

Test task: "Audit this service page. Provide a list of on-page SEO improvements (title tag, headers, keyword density, internal links, schema)."

🛠️ Hire on-page SEO specialists →

5. Link Building Outreach

What they do: Find guest post opportunities, send personalized outreach emails, follow up with prospects, negotiate placements, and track responses.

Hourly rate: $8-14/hour

Skill level: Mid-level

Tools needed: Ahrefs, Hunter.io, Gmass or Mailshake

What to look for: Strong written communication, persistence, ability to personalize outreach (not generic templates), understanding of DR/DA metrics.

Test task: "Find 10 guest post opportunities in the home improvement niche (DR 30+). Write a personalized outreach email for one of them."

6. Local Citation Building

What they do: Submit business information (NAP: Name, Address, Phone) to local directories, verify listings, fix inconsistencies, and track submissions.

Hourly rate: $5-8/hour

Skill level: Entry-level

Tools needed: BrightLocal, Whitespark, or manual submissions

What to look for: Attention to detail (NAP consistency is critical), ability to follow SOPs, patience for repetitive work.

Test task: "Submit this business to 10 local directories. Provide a spreadsheet with directory name, URL, login credentials, and submission status."

7. Google Business Profile Management

What they do: Post weekly updates, respond to reviews, upload photos, optimize business description, add products/services, and monitor insights.

Hourly rate: $6-10/hour

Skill level: Entry to mid-level

Tools needed: Google Business Profile dashboard

What to look for: Understanding of local SEO signals, creativity with GBP posts, ability to respond professionally to negative reviews.

Test task: "Create 4 GBP posts for a roofing company (1 offer, 1 update, 1 how-to, 1 seasonal). Write a professional response to this 2-star review."

8. Competitor Analysis

What they do: Analyze competitor websites, identify their top-ranking pages, backlink sources, content gaps, and keyword strategies.

Hourly rate: $8-12/hour

Skill level: Mid-level

Tools needed: Ahrefs, SEMrush, SimilarWeb

What to look for: Analytical thinking, ability to extract actionable insights (not just data dumps), understanding of competitive gaps.

Test task: "Analyze these 3 competitors. Identify: (1) their top 10 ranking pages, (2) 5 keywords we should target that they rank for, (3) their primary backlink sources."

9. Technical SEO Audits

What they do: Crawl websites with Screaming Frog, identify technical issues (broken links, duplicate content, slow pages), check schema markup, analyze Core Web Vitals.

Hourly rate: $12-18/hour

Skill level: Mid to senior-level

Tools needed: Screaming Frog, Google Search Console, PageSpeed Insights

What to look for: Strong technical understanding, ability to interpret crawl data, experience with schema markup and site speed optimization.

Test task: "Crawl this site with Screaming Frog. Provide a prioritized list of technical SEO issues (broken links, missing alt text, duplicate titles, crawl errors)."

10. Reporting and Analytics

What they do: Pull data from Google Analytics, Search Console, and rank trackers. Create monthly reports showing traffic, rankings, conversions, and top-performing content.

Hourly rate: $8-12/hour

Skill level: Mid-level

Tools needed: Google Analytics 4, Search Console, Data Studio (Looker Studio)

What to look for: Data literacy, ability to visualize metrics clearly, understanding of what matters to clients (traffic, leads, revenue — not just vanity metrics).

Test task: "Create a one-page SEO report for this client. Include: organic traffic (vs last month), top 10 ranking keywords, top 5 landing pages, and 3 actionable recommendations."

11. Image Optimization

What they do: Compress images for faster loading, rename files with keywords, add descriptive alt text, convert to WebP format, and upload to WordPress.

Hourly rate: $5-8/hour

Skill level: Entry-level

Tools needed: TinyPNG, ShortPixel, or ImageOptim

What to look for: Understanding of image SEO best practices, basic photo editing skills (cropping, resizing), attention to file naming conventions.

Test task: "Optimize these 10 images: compress to under 100KB each, rename with relevant keywords, add descriptive alt text, and convert to WebP."

How to Structure VA Roles

You don't need to hire 11 different VAs. Most can handle 2-4 related tasks:

Content VA: Keyword research + writing + uploading (15-20 hours/week, $8-12/hour)

Link Building VA: Outreach + guest posting + citation building (10-15 hours/week, $8-14/hour)

Technical VA: On-page optimization + audits + schema markup (10-15 hours/week, $12-18/hour)

Admin VA: GBP management + reporting + image optimization (5-10 hours/week, $6-10/hour)

Start with one VA handling content, then expand as you scale.

Sample Job Description (Content VA)

Title: SEO Content Writer (Part-Time, Remote)

Rate: $8-12/hour (based on experience)

Hours: 15-20 hours/week

Responsibilities:

  • Research keywords using Ahrefs (training provided)
  • Write 4-6 SEO-optimized blog posts per month (1,500-2,000 words each)
  • Upload and format posts in WordPress with proper headings and internal links
  • Optimize meta descriptions and image alt text
  • Follow content briefs and brand guidelines

Requirements:

  • Fluent English (written and spoken)
  • 2+ years SEO writing experience
  • Portfolio with published blog posts
  • Experience with WordPress and Yoast SEO
  • Familiarity with SurferSEO or similar tools (preferred)

To apply: Send your resume, 3 writing samples, and answer this: "What's the difference between informational and transactional keywords? Give 2 examples of each."

Final Thoughts

The fastest way to scale your SEO output is to delegate the execution and focus on strategy. A good VA can handle 80% of these tasks at $6-15/hour, freeing up your time for client acquisition, strategy calls, and high-level work.

Start with one VA handling content or link building. Once you have SOPs in place and see the ROI, expand to a full team.

Most successful agencies and consultants aren't doing this work themselves — they're managing VAs who execute at a fraction of the cost.
