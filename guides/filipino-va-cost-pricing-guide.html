How much does a Filipino virtual assistant cost in 2026?
Filipino VAs cost between $600 and $2,500+ per month depending on the hiring model. Freelancers range from $600-$900/month, managed facility-based VAs from $1,200-$2,500/month, including infrastructure, supervision, and backup systems.
The wide range reflects what's included beyond just salary. A $600/month freelancer on OnlineJobs.ph is self-managed with no infrastructure guarantees. A $2,000/month managed VA includes office space, dual ISP, generator backup, on-site management, payroll processing, equipment, and guaranteed replacement — effectively a plug-and-play team member.

What is the hourly rate for a Filipino virtual assistant?
Entry-level Filipino VAs charge $3-$5/hour. Mid-level specialists range from $5-$8/hour. Senior and executive VAs charge $8-$15/hour depending on skills, experience, and whether infrastructure is included.
Hourly rates are most common on freelance platforms. However, most managed providers use monthly pricing because it reflects the full service — not just the worker's time. A $1,500/month managed VA at 160 hours works out to roughly $9.40/hour, but that rate includes office space, dual ISP, generator backup, management, and HR support that a freelancer at $5/hour does not provide.

Why are some Filipino VAs more expensive than others?
Higher-cost VAs include managed infrastructure — professional office space, dual ISP, generator backup, on-site supervision, pre-training, HR/payroll, and guaranteed replacement. Lower-cost freelancers are self-managed with no infrastructure guarantees.
The difference isn't just the worker's skill — it's the operational layer around them. A freelancer working from home during typhoon season may disappear for days. A facility-based VA has redundant internet and generator power. A freelancer may secretly work multiple jobs (moonlighting). A facility-based VA is supervised during set hours. You're paying for certainty, not just labor.
